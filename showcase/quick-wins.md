# Quick Wins

Things you can try in 5 minutes to see what palimpsest looks like in practice.

---

## 1. Generate a Stakeholder Update

**Prompt:**
```
Review my program dashboard and draft a weekly stakeholder update.
Focus on what changed this week, current blockers, and decisions needed.
```

**What you get:** A structured update ready to post, with status, metrics, and risks pulled from your program state.

**Why it's useful:** Cuts update writing from 45 minutes to 5 minutes of review and editing.

## 2. Assess Launch Readiness

**Prompt:**
```
Based on the current program state, fill out a go/no-go assessment.
Flag anything that's not ready and suggest what needs to happen before launch.
```

**What you get:** A structured readiness matrix with honest status assessments and gap identification.

**Why it's useful:** Forces a systematic review instead of "I think we're ready."

## 3. Draft a Decision Record

**Prompt:**
```
We need to decide between [Option A] and [Option B] for [topic].
Document the trade-offs, make a recommendation, and format it as a decision record.
```

**What you get:** A decision log entry with context, options, rationale, and reversibility assessment.

**Why it's useful:** Captures the "why" while it's still fresh, prevents future re-litigation.

## 4. Create a Risk Assessment

**Prompt:**
```
Review the current blockers and in-progress items.
Identify risks, rate their likelihood and impact, and suggest mitigations.
```

**What you get:** A risk table with actionable mitigations, prioritized by severity.

**Why it's useful:** Surfaces risks you might be carrying in your head but haven't written down.

## 5. Build an Onboarding Brief

**Prompt:**
```
A new team member is joining next week. Create an onboarding brief that covers:
what the program is, current state, key people, where to find things.
```

**What you get:** A concise onboarding document that saves the new person hours of context-gathering.

**Why it's useful:** New people ramp faster, and you don't have to repeat yourself.

## 6. Summarize a Week of Activity

**Prompt:**
```
Summarize what happened across the program this week.
Group by workstream, highlight decisions made, and note anything that shifted.
```

**What you get:** A synthesized view of the week that connects dots across workstreams.

**Why it's useful:** Gives you the big picture when you've been heads-down in details.

## 7. Audit Your Documentation

**Prompt:**
```
Review the document registry. Flag anything that hasn't been updated in 30+ days,
anything without a clear owner, and any gaps in coverage.
```

**What you get:** A documentation health report with specific action items.

**Why it's useful:** Prevents documentation rot before it becomes a problem.

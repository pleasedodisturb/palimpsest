# Remote Onboarding

A structured approach to onboarding someone into a distributed program without relying on "just sit next to someone and absorb context."

---

## The Challenge

Remote onboarding fails when it's treated as a checklist of tools to set up and documents to read. The real challenge is building mental models: how does this program work, who knows what, where do I find things, and what's the culture here?

## Phase 0: Before They Start (Week -1)

### Goals

- New person can hit the ground running on Day 1
- No first-day friction on access or tooling

### Activities

| Task | Owner | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Create accounts and grant access | [OWNER] | All tools, repos, channels |
| Prepare onboarding document | [OWNER] | Program overview, key links, who's who |
| Schedule Week 1 meetings | [OWNER] | 1:1s with key people, no group meetings yet |
| Assign an onboarding buddy | [OWNER] | Someone available for quick questions |
| Share pre-reading (limited) | [OWNER] | 3-5 essential docs, not 30 |

### Pre-Reading Package

Keep it short. These are the documents someone should read before Day 1:

1. Program overview (what are we doing and why)
2. Team structure (who does what)
3. Current status (where are we right now)
4. Communication norms (how we work)

## Phase 1: Orient (Week 1)

### Goals

- Understand the program's purpose, current state, and key people
- Know where to find information
- Make one small contribution

### Activities

- Read the program documentation (guided, not dumped)
- 1:1 meetings with 3-5 key people (25 min each)
- Shadow one working session or review
- Complete one small, well-defined task
- Write the first weekly update (use the [first-weekly-update template](../templates/first-weekly-update.md))

### Success Criteria

- Can explain the program's purpose and current phase to someone else
- Knows who to ask for what
- Has committed one change or contribution
- Has posted in the team channel

## Phase 2: Contribute (Weeks 2-3)

### Goals

- Take ownership of a defined workstream
- Build working relationships with the team
- Identify gaps in documentation or process

### Activities

- Own a workstream or deliverable (with support)
- Attend regular team ceremonies
- Start writing regular status updates
- Identify one improvement and propose it
- Document anything that was hard to find or understand

### Success Criteria

- Independently driving a workstream
- Contributing to team discussions
- Writing updates without heavy editing
- Has improved at least one document or process

## Phase 3: Integrate (Weeks 3-4)

### Goals

- Operating at full capacity within their role
- Contributing to program direction, not just execution

### Activities

- Full workstream ownership without hand-holding
- Participating in cross-team coordination
- Mentoring the next person (if applicable)
- Retrospective on the onboarding experience

### Success Criteria

- Other team members come to them for their area of expertise
- Contributing to decisions, not just executing them
- Can onboard the next person

## Common Pitfalls

- **Information dump on Day 1** — sharing 50 documents creates anxiety, not context. Curate ruthlessly.
- **No buddy system** — a named person for "stupid questions" dramatically reduces ramp time.
- **Skipping 1:1s** — reading docs tells you what the program is; talking to people tells you how it actually works.
- **No early wins** — if someone doesn't contribute something tangible in Week 1, they'll feel like a spectator.
- **Assuming they'll ask** — new people don't know what they don't know. Check in proactively.
- **Onboarding as a one-time event** — it's a 4-week process, not a Day 1 event.

## Onboarding Checklist

```markdown
### Week 0
- [ ] Access to all tools and systems
- [ ] Onboarding document shared
- [ ] Buddy assigned
- [ ] Week 1 meetings scheduled
- [ ] Pre-reading sent

### Week 1
- [ ] Program docs reviewed
- [ ] Key 1:1s completed
- [ ] First contribution made
- [ ] First update written
- [ ] Questions documented

### Week 2-3
- [ ] Workstream assigned
- [ ] Regular updates started
- [ ] One improvement proposed
- [ ] Documentation gaps filled

### Week 4
- [ ] Full ownership demonstrated
- [ ] Onboarding retro completed
- [ ] Feedback collected
```
